BIG TONA

Big Tona is a lyrical force and cultural icon reshaping the sound of modern hip hop. With gritty storytelling and razor-sharp delivery, he’s earned his stripes as a respected voice from the underground to the mainstage. A Toronto trailblazer, Big Tona has shared the stage with legends like Nas and Ghostface Killah. His critically acclaimed albums Black Mirror and The Ivy League showcase a masterclass in raw lyricism. Tona was crowned a JUNO Award winner for Rap Recording of the Year as part of Naturally Born Strangers. He's been featured on BET, Complex, Sway in the Morning, and CBC Music. Known for his punchy flow and socially charged bars, his pen never pulls punches. Tona’s music bridges street narratives with elevated thought — a voice for the now and next. His latest project delivers timeless beats and sharp conviction, proving he's far from done. Big Tona doesn’t just rap — he builds legacy through sound.

LALove The Boss

LALove The Boss is a fearless powerhouse blending bars, beauty, and business. Hailing from Maryland, she’s built a brand that’s as unshakable as her flow. Her viral freestyle videos have amassed millions of views across Instagram and TikTok. With a sound that fuses hip hop grit and southern flair, she’s redefining femme rap on her own terms. A social media juggernaut, LALove boasts over 1 million followers and counting. She’s been featured in campaigns for Fashion Nova, Revolt, and Rolling Out Magazine. Her debut single “Get Money” set the tone — unapologetic, bold, and bossed up. She’s performed live across major U.S. cities, turning stages into her personal runway. More than an artist, she’s a movement — inspiring women to own their voice and vision. LALove The Boss isn’t next — she’s now.

Moe Music

Moe Music is a genre-blending vocalist known for smooth melodies and soulful depth. Hailing from New York, he fuses R&B, hip hop, and gospel into a sound that’s rich and timeless. His debut EP Moments in Time gained traction on Apple Music and Spotify editorial playlists. With heartfelt hooks and real-life lyrics, Moe connects deeply with every listener. He’s performed live at SOBs, Brooklyn Bowl, and opened for artists like Eric Bellinger and BJ The Chicago Kid. Moe’s single “Letter to My Father” became a viral anthem for fatherhood and healing. He’s been featured in Essence, Vibe, and on Hot 97’s Who’s Next platform. From underground stages to national spotlights, Moe is building a loyal fanbase one verse at a time. His voice cuts through the noise — warm, powerful, and unforgettable. Moe Music isn’t chasing trends — he’s creating timeless moments.

Teemanay

Teemanay is the Afro-fusion prince bringing global vibes with street soul. Born in Nigeria and based in New York, he fuses Afrobeats, dancehall, and R&B into pure energy. A Grammy-nominated artist, Teemanay is elevating the genre with global recognition. His breakout single Wole racked up over 1 million streams and caught attention worldwide. He’s been featured on MTV Base Africa, Audiomack Trending, and OkayAfrica. Teemanay has performed alongside Afrobeat giants like Davido, Burna Boy, and Wande Coal. Known for his infectious hooks and electrifying stage presence, he commands every room he steps in. He’s built a loyal fanbase across Africa, the U.S., and the Caribbean with nonstop momentum. From viral dance challenges to sold-out showcases, his wave is undeniable. Teemanay isn’t following the Afrobeat movement — he’s leading it.

SNACO

SNACO is Georgia’s next-up spitter making undeniable noise in the new wave of hip hop. With a gritty Southern cadence and razor-sharp bars, he’s carving a name all his own. A newcomer with momentum, SNACO has already built a loyal following of 14,000+ fans and counting. His singles like Demon Time, Cash App, and Whiplash are heating up playlists and timelines. Unfiltered and relentless, his music hits with raw energy and real-life conviction. SNACO’s visuals match his bars — bold, cinematic, and built for replay. He’s been featured across indie blogs, TikTok trends, and underground Spotify playlists. From street anthems to trunk-rattling bangers, his sound is built for the grind and the glow-up. Each drop grows his audience and sharpens his reputation as one to watch. SNACO is building his own lane, bar for bar.
